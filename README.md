# City-Employee-Earnings-Dashboard
# Objective
<div align="justify">
Developed an interactive Power BI dashboard to analyze city employee compensation, overtime trends, workforce distribution, and department-level payroll insights.The dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor payroll expenses, identify overtime-heavy departments, and support workforce planning decisions through data-driven analytics.
</div>

# Skills Utilized
* Power BI Dashboard Development
* DAX Measure Creation
* Data Modeling
* Power Query (ETL)
* Time Intelligence Functions
* Data Cleaning & Transformation
* Interactive Report Design
* KPI Development
* Trend Analysis
* Data Visualization & Storytelling

# Approach
## Data Preparation
* Cleaned and transformed raw employee earnings data using Power Query.
* Created a structured data model with relationships between the employee earnings table and date table.
  <br> Generated calculated columns such as:
* Year
* Quarter
* YearQuarter
* QuarterStartDate
* KPI & Measure Development

Developed key DAX measures including:
* Total Earnings
* Average Earnings
* Employee Count
* Overtime %
* Base Pay
* Overtime Pay
* Longevity Pay
* Miscellaneous Pay
* Post-Separation Pay
* Dashboard Design

Designed a multi-page interactive dashboard with:

* Workforce Overview
* Department & Pay Analysis
* Employee Insights
* Interactivity

Implemented dynamic slicers and filters for:

* Year
* Quarter
* Department
* Employee Category
* Salary Type

<b> Visualization Strategy</b>

Used KPI cards, line charts, donut charts, stacked bars, and employee-level tables to deliver clear and actionable workforce insights.

# Key Insights
### Workforce Compensation Growth
* Total employee earnings reached approximately $16 billion.
* Compensation expenses showed strong growth after 2023.
### Workforce Expansion
* Employee count increased to around 54K employees by 2025.
* Indicates workforce scaling and increased operational demand.
### High Overtime Dependency
* Overtime contributes 12.4% of total compensation.
* Police and Fire departments showed the highest overtime spending.
### Dominance of Civil Service Employees
* Around 70% of employees belong to the Civil Service category.
* Exempt employees account for a smaller workforce share.
### Public Safety Departments Drive Payroll Costs
* Police and Fire departments contribute the largest share of compensation expenses.
* These departments also show significant overtime dependency.
### Earnings Exceed Base Salaries
Actual employee earnings are substantially higher than annual base salaries due to:
* Overtime
* Longevity Pay
* Miscellaneous Compensation
* Post-Separation Pay
* High-Cost Job Titles

Top earning roles include:

* Police Officer
* Firefighter
* Correctional Officer
* Detective
* Medical Specialist

# Recommendations
### Reduce Overtime Dependency
* Optimize staffing allocation and scheduling in overtime-heavy departments.
* Improve workforce planning to reduce excessive overtime costs.
### Strengthen Payroll Monitoring
* Monitor departments with rapidly increasing compensation trends.
* Implement department-level compensation KPIs.
### Improve Workforce Planning
* Use historical trends to forecast staffing and payroll requirements.
* Identify departments requiring hiring expansion.
### Enhance Compensation Transparency
* Separate recurring and non-recurring compensation components for better financial tracking.
### Focus on Cost Optimization
* Analyze operational inefficiencies in departments with high overtime spending.
* Evaluate workload balancing strategies.
